What Is Unsecured Claim in Bankruptcy?

Home › Glossary › Unsecured Claim

An unsecured claim is a creditor's right to payment that is not backed by collateral or a lien on the debtor's property. Common unsecured claims include credit card debt, medical bills, personal loans, and most lawsuit judgments. Unsecured creditors are last in line for payment and often receive little or nothing in bankruptcy.

How It Works

Unsecured claims are divided into two categories: priority unsecured claims and general unsecured claims. Priority claims (taxes, support obligations, wages) are paid first and in full. General unsecured claims are paid from whatever remains after secured and priority claims are satisfied.

In Chapter 7, general unsecured creditors receive a distribution only in asset cases -- and even then, the distribution is often pennies on the dollar. In the approximately 95% of cases that are no-asset, unsecured creditors receive nothing. The remaining debt is discharged.

In Chapter 13, unsecured creditors receive whatever the debtor's disposable income can support over the 3-5 year plan, subject to the best interests test (at least as much as they would receive in Chapter 7). The percentage paid to unsecured creditors varies widely -- from 0% to 100% -- depending on the debtor's income and expenses.

Key Facts

  • Not backed by collateral -- the creditor has no property to seize
  • General unsecured claims are lowest priority in distribution
  • In Chapter 7 no-asset cases, unsecured creditors receive nothing
  • In Chapter 13, payment ranges from 0% to 100% depending on disposable income
  • Most unsecured debt is dischargeable (credit cards, medical, personal loans)

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between secured and unsecured claims?

A secured claim is backed by collateral (a mortgage is secured by the house, a car loan by the vehicle). An unsecured claim has no collateral. If the debtor defaults on a secured debt, the creditor can seize the collateral. Unsecured creditors have no such right.

Will credit card companies get anything from my bankruptcy?

In most Chapter 7 cases, no. In Chapter 13, credit card companies receive whatever percentage the plan pays to general unsecured creditors. This can range from 0% to 100% depending on your disposable income and the best interests test.

Can unsecured debt become secured?

Yes, if the creditor obtains a judgment and records a judgment lien against the debtor's property. This is why timely bankruptcy filing can be important -- filing before a creditor obtains a judgment lien keeps the debt unsecured and easier to discharge.
