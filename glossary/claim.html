What Is Claim in Bankruptcy?

Home › Glossary › Claim

A claim in bankruptcy is a creditor's right to payment from the debtor or the bankruptcy estate. Claims are classified as secured, unsecured priority, or general unsecured, and the classification determines the order and amount of payment the creditor receives in the bankruptcy case.

How It Works

Creditors assert claims by filing a proof of claim form with the bankruptcy court. The claim must include the amount owed, the basis for the claim, and any supporting documentation. In Chapter 7 no-asset cases, creditors are not required to file proofs of claim because there is nothing to distribute.

Claims are classified into three main categories. Secured claims are backed by collateral (mortgages, car loans) and are paid first from the collateral's value. Priority claims (taxes, domestic support obligations, administrative expenses) are paid before general unsecured claims. General unsecured claims (credit cards, medical bills) are paid last and often receive only pennies on the dollar.

The debtor or trustee can object to a claim if the amount is wrong, the claim is untimely, or the claim is otherwise invalid. Disputed claims are resolved by the bankruptcy court. The bar date -- the deadline for filing proofs of claim -- is set by the court and strictly enforced.

Key Facts

  • Filed on Official Form 410 (Proof of Claim) with supporting documentation
  • Three main types: secured, priority unsecured, and general unsecured
  • The bar date is the deadline for filing -- late claims may be disallowed
  • The debtor or trustee can object to any claim
  • In Chapter 7 no-asset cases, claims do not need to be filed

Frequently Asked Questions

What if a creditor does not file a claim?

If a creditor does not file a proof of claim by the bar date, the claim may be disallowed and the creditor receives nothing from the estate. In Chapter 13, the debtor can file a claim on behalf of the creditor.

Can I dispute a creditor's claim?

Yes. You can file an objection to a claim if the amount is incorrect, the debt is not owed, or the claim was filed late. The court will schedule a hearing to resolve the objection.

What is the difference between a claim and a debt?

All debts are potential claims, but a claim specifically refers to the formal assertion of a right to payment in the bankruptcy case. A debt becomes a claim when the creditor files a proof of claim or the debt is scheduled by the debtor.
