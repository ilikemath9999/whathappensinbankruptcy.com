What Is Petition Preparer in Bankruptcy?

Home › Glossary › Petition Preparer

A petition preparer is a non-attorney who assists debtors in preparing bankruptcy forms and documents for filing. Petition preparers can type forms and organize paperwork but cannot provide legal advice, explain legal options, or represent the debtor in court. They are regulated by 11 U.S.C. Section 110.

How It Works

Petition preparers fill a gap between full attorney representation and completely self-represented filing. They help pro se debtors complete the official bankruptcy forms -- the petition, schedules, means test, and statement of financial affairs -- by typing the debtor's information into the correct forms.

Section 110 strictly prohibits petition preparers from offering legal advice. They cannot tell the debtor which chapter to file, how to value property, which exemptions to claim, or how to handle specific legal issues. They can only provide typing services. Petition preparers must sign all documents they prepare, include their name and Social Security number, and charge reasonable fees.

Violations of Section 110 can result in fines up to $500 per violation, disgorgement of fees, and injunctions. Petition preparers who provide unauthorized legal advice (practicing law without a license) face additional penalties under state law.

Key Facts

  • Regulated by 11 U.S.C. Section 110
  • Cannot provide legal advice -- typing services only
  • Must sign all documents prepared and disclose fees charged
  • Fees are subject to court review and can be disgorged if unreasonable
  • Violations can result in fines, fee disgorgement, and injunctions

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I use a petition preparer instead of a lawyer?

A petition preparer costs less than an attorney ($100-$400 vs. $1,500-$3,500) but cannot advise you on any legal issues. If your case is straightforward, a preparer may be sufficient. If you have property to protect, debts to challenge, or complex income, an attorney is strongly recommended.

How much does a petition preparer charge?

Fees typically range from $100 to $400. The court can review the fee and order disgorgement if it is unreasonable. This is significantly less than attorney fees but comes with the limitation of no legal advice.

Can a petition preparer represent me in court?

No. Petition preparers cannot appear in court, file motions, or represent you in any legal proceeding. Only attorneys can represent debtors in bankruptcy court. If issues arise during your case, you would need to hire an attorney or handle them pro se.
