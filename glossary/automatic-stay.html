What Is Automatic Stay in Bankruptcy?

Home › Glossary › Automatic Stay

The automatic stay is an injunction that takes effect the instant a bankruptcy petition is filed, halting virtually all collection activity against the debtor. It stops lawsuits, foreclosures, repossessions, wage garnishments, utility shutoffs, and creditor phone calls immediately and automatically.

How It Works

The automatic stay is one of the most powerful protections in bankruptcy law, established by 11 U.S.C. Section 362. It does not require a court order -- it activates automatically when the petition is filed. Any creditor who continues collection activity after the stay takes effect may be held in contempt and liable for damages.

The stay protects the debtor and the bankruptcy estate. It gives the debtor breathing room to reorganize finances and gives the trustee time to administer the estate. In Chapter 13, the stay allows the debtor to propose a repayment plan without creditors racing to seize assets.

The stay has important exceptions: it does not stop criminal proceedings, certain tax proceedings, domestic support obligation collections, or evictions where the landlord already obtained a judgment before filing. Secured creditors can also seek relief from the stay by filing a motion if they are not being adequately protected.

Key Facts

  • Takes effect instantly when the bankruptcy petition is filed -- no court order needed
  • Stops lawsuits, garnishments, foreclosures, repossessions, and collection calls
  • Violations can result in contempt, actual damages, and attorney fees
  • Repeat filers may receive a limited stay (30 days) or no stay at all under Section 362(c)
  • Secured creditors can request relief from the stay by filing a motion

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the automatic stay last?

In Chapter 7, the stay lasts until the case is closed, dismissed, or the debtor receives a discharge (typically 3-4 months). In Chapter 13, the stay remains in effect throughout the 3-5 year plan. Secured creditors can seek early termination by filing a motion for relief.

What happens if a creditor violates the automatic stay?

The creditor may be held in contempt of court and ordered to pay actual damages, including attorney fees and emotional distress damages. Willful violations under Section 362(k) allow the debtor to recover punitive damages in some circuits.

Does the automatic stay stop evictions?

It depends on timing. If the landlord obtained a judgment for possession before the bankruptcy was filed, the stay generally does not apply. If no judgment exists, the stay temporarily halts eviction proceedings.
