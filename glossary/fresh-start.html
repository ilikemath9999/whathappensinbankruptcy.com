What Is Fresh Start in Bankruptcy?

Home › Glossary › Fresh Start

The fresh start is the fundamental purpose of bankruptcy law -- giving honest but unfortunate debtors a new beginning free from the burden of overwhelming debt. The Supreme Court has described the fresh start as 'a new opportunity in life, unhampered by the pressure and discouragement of pre-existing debt.'

How It Works

The fresh start doctrine is not a single statute but a guiding principle woven throughout bankruptcy law. The discharge eliminates legal liability for pre-bankruptcy debts. The discharge injunction prevents creditors from collecting discharged debts. Exemptions allow the debtor to keep essential property. Together, these protections give the debtor a clean financial slate.

The fresh start is available only to 'honest but unfortunate debtors.' Debtors who commit fraud, conceal assets, or abuse the bankruptcy system may be denied a discharge under Section 727(a). The means test under Section 707(b) prevents abuse of Chapter 7 by debtors who can afford to repay their debts.

Research shows that the fresh start works. Studies of debtors who receive a discharge show significant improvements in credit scores, home ownership rates, and overall financial stability within 2-3 years. The ability to file bankruptcy also encourages entrepreneurship by reducing the personal risk of business failure.

Key Facts

  • The Supreme Court has described the fresh start as bankruptcy's 'central purpose'
  • Available only to 'honest but unfortunate' debtors -- fraud disqualifies
  • Implemented through the discharge, discharge injunction, and exemptions
  • Credit scores typically begin recovering within 12-18 months of discharge
  • The fresh start enables entrepreneurship by limiting personal risk

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to rebuild after bankruptcy?

Most people see meaningful credit improvement within 12-18 months. FHA mortgages are available 2 years after Chapter 7 discharge. New credit cards (secured) are often available within weeks of discharge.

Can I file bankruptcy more than once?

Yes, but there are time limits. You must wait 8 years between Chapter 7 discharges, 6 years between a Chapter 7 and Chapter 13, and 2 years between Chapter 13 discharges. These are measured from filing date to filing date.

Will employers know I filed bankruptcy?

Bankruptcy filings are public records, but most employers do not check bankruptcy records. Federal law prohibits government employers from discriminating based on bankruptcy. Private employer protections vary by state and circuit.
