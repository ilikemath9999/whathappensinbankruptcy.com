What Is Trustee in Bankruptcy?

Home › Glossary › Trustee

A trustee in bankruptcy is an individual appointed to administer the bankruptcy estate. There are several types: Chapter 7 panel trustees who liquidate assets, Chapter 13 standing trustees who manage repayment plans, Chapter 11 trustees (appointed only for cause), and the U.S. Trustee who oversees the bankruptcy system.

How It Works

The U.S. Trustee Program, a component of the Department of Justice, appoints and supervises bankruptcy trustees. Chapter 7 panel trustees serve on a rotating basis and are assigned cases as they are filed. Chapter 13 standing trustees handle all Chapter 13 cases in their assigned division. Both are private individuals, not government employees.

The trustee's duties include: conducting the 341 meeting, examining the debtor's financial affairs, objecting to exemptions or discharge when appropriate, recovering avoidable transfers, liquidating non-exempt assets (Chapter 7), collecting and distributing plan payments (Chapter 13), and filing final reports with the court.

The U.S. Trustee plays a separate, supervisory role. The U.S. Trustee does not administer individual cases but oversees the system: appointing panel and standing trustees, monitoring case progress, filing abuse motions under Section 707(b), reviewing fee applications, and ensuring compliance with the Bankruptcy Code.

Key Facts

  • Chapter 7 panel trustees: assigned by rotation, liquidate non-exempt assets
  • Chapter 13 standing trustees: handle all cases in a division, manage plan payments
  • U.S. Trustee: DOJ component, oversees the bankruptcy system (not individual cases)
  • Trustees are compensated from estate funds (percentage-based)
  • A Chapter 11 trustee replaces the debtor in possession only for cause

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the trustee working for the creditors?

The trustee is a neutral fiduciary whose duty is to the estate and its creditors collectively. They do not represent any individual creditor but administer the case for the benefit of all parties according to the priorities established by the Bankruptcy Code.

How does the trustee get paid?

Chapter 7 trustees receive a $60 fee per no-asset case. In asset cases, they earn a percentage of distributions: 25% of the first $5,000, 10% of the next $45,000, 5% of the next $950,000, and 3% of amounts above $1,000,000. Chapter 13 trustees receive a percentage of plan payments (typically 7-10%).

Can I replace my trustee?

Not in the usual course. Trustees are assigned, not chosen. A trustee can be removed only for cause -- misconduct, incompetence, or conflict of interest. This is extremely rare.
