What Is Meeting of Creditors in Bankruptcy?

Home › Glossary › Meeting of Creditors

The meeting of creditors, also called the 341 meeting, is a mandatory hearing in every bankruptcy case where the debtor is questioned under oath by the trustee and any creditors who attend. Despite its name, creditors rarely appear in consumer cases. The meeting typically lasts 5 to 15 minutes.

How It Works

The meeting of creditors is required by 11 U.S.C. Section 341(a) and is scheduled approximately 21-40 days after the petition is filed. The debtor must attend, present government-issued photo identification and proof of Social Security number, and answer questions about the accuracy and completeness of the bankruptcy schedules.

The trustee conducts the meeting and asks standard questions: Did you list all your assets? Did you list all your debts? Have you made any transfers of property in the last few years? Is all the information in your petition true and correct? The trustee may also ask follow-up questions about specific assets, income, or transactions.

Creditors have the right to attend and ask questions, but in consumer cases they almost never do. The meeting is not held in a courtroom and the judge does not attend. After the meeting, creditors have 60 days to file complaints objecting to the debtor's discharge or the dischargeability of specific debts.

Key Facts

  • Required by 11 U.S.C. Section 341(a) in every bankruptcy case
  • Scheduled 21-40 days after filing
  • The debtor testifies under oath
  • No judge is present -- the trustee runs the meeting
  • Creditors rarely attend in consumer cases

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a lawyer at the 341 meeting?

If you have an attorney, they will attend with you. If you are pro se, you attend alone. Having an attorney is helpful because they can prepare you for questions, but many pro se debtors successfully navigate the meeting.

What documents do I need to bring?

You must bring a government-issued photo ID (driver's license, passport) and proof of your Social Security number (Social Security card, tax return, W-2). Some trustees require recent pay stubs and bank statements as well.

Can the 341 meeting be done by phone?

Many districts now allow telephonic or video 341 meetings, especially since COVID-19. Check with your local court or trustee for the current policy. Some districts have returned to in-person meetings while others remain virtual.
