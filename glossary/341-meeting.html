What Is 341 Meeting in Bankruptcy?

Home › Glossary › 341 Meeting

A 341 meeting, also called a meeting of creditors, is a mandatory hearing in every bankruptcy case where the debtor answers questions under oath from the bankruptcy trustee and any creditors who choose to attend. It typically lasts 5 to 15 minutes and takes place about 30 days after filing.

How It Works

The 341 meeting is required by 11 U.S.C. Section 341. The debtor must appear (in person or by phone/video, depending on the district) and present valid photo identification and proof of Social Security number. The trustee verifies the debtor's identity and asks questions about the bankruptcy schedules, assets, income, and expenses.

Creditors may attend and ask questions, but they rarely do in consumer cases. The meeting is not held before a judge -- it takes place in a meeting room or conference setting. If the trustee needs additional documents, the meeting may be continued to a later date.

Failure to appear at the 341 meeting can result in dismissal of the bankruptcy case. The debtor's attorney (if represented) typically attends as well. After the meeting concludes, creditors have 60 days to file objections to discharge in Chapter 7 cases.

Key Facts

  • Required in every bankruptcy case -- Chapter 7, 11, 12, and 13
  • Usually held 21-40 days after the petition is filed
  • The debtor testifies under oath and must bring photo ID and Social Security proof
  • Creditors may attend but rarely do in consumer cases
  • The bankruptcy judge does not attend the 341 meeting

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens if I miss my 341 meeting?

If you miss your 341 meeting without requesting a continuance, the court will likely dismiss your bankruptcy case. Most courts allow one rescheduling for good cause.

Can creditors ask me anything at the 341 meeting?

Creditors can ask questions related to your financial affairs, assets, debts, and the accuracy of your schedules. However, the trustee controls the meeting and can limit irrelevant or harassing questions.

How long does a 341 meeting last?

Most 341 meetings last between 5 and 15 minutes. Complex cases or cases where the trustee requests additional documentation may require a continued meeting.
