What Is Stay Violation in Bankruptcy?

Home › Glossary › Stay Violation

A stay violation occurs when a creditor takes collection action against the debtor or the debtor's property after the automatic stay has taken effect. Stay violations can include phone calls, collection letters, lawsuits, foreclosures, repossessions, wage garnishments, and bank account freezes.

How It Works

The automatic stay under Section 362 prohibits virtually all collection activity from the moment the bankruptcy petition is filed. Any creditor who continues collection activity after the stay takes effect -- even unknowingly -- violates the stay. Willful violations under Section 362(k) entitle the debtor to actual damages, costs, attorney fees, and in some cases punitive damages.

Common stay violations include: a creditor filing a lawsuit after the bankruptcy was filed, continuing a garnishment, repossessing a vehicle, attempting foreclosure, calling or writing to collect a debt, offsetting a bank account, or reporting the debt as delinquent rather than 'included in bankruptcy' on credit reports.

The debtor can seek relief by filing a motion for contempt or a motion for damages under Section 362(k). The debtor must prove the creditor knew about the bankruptcy (had notice) and the action was willful -- meaning the creditor intentionally performed the act, not that the creditor intended to violate the stay. Damages can include out-of-pocket costs, attorney fees, emotional distress, and punitive damages.

Key Facts

  • Governed by 11 U.S.C. Section 362(k)
  • Willful violations entitle the debtor to actual damages, costs, and attorney fees
  • The creditor must have had notice of the bankruptcy filing
  • 'Willful' means the act was intentional -- not that the creditor intended to violate the stay
  • Actions taken in violation of the stay are generally void or voidable

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I do if a creditor violates the stay?

Document the violation (save letters, record dates of calls, take screenshots). Notify the creditor in writing that you have filed bankruptcy and provide the case number. If the violations continue, consult your attorney about filing a motion for contempt and damages.

Can I get money for a stay violation?

Yes. Under Section 362(k), you can recover actual damages (out-of-pocket costs, attorney fees) and in egregious cases, punitive damages. Some courts also award emotional distress damages for willful violations.

Are actions taken in violation of the stay valid?

Most courts hold that actions taken in violation of the automatic stay are void (have no legal effect) rather than voidable. This means a foreclosure sale or judgment entered in violation of the stay is automatically invalid.
