What Is Redemption in Bankruptcy?

Home › Glossary › Redemption

Redemption in bankruptcy allows a Chapter 7 debtor to keep secured personal property by paying the creditor the current fair market value of the collateral in a single lump sum payment, even if the amount owed on the loan is higher. It is an alternative to reaffirmation or surrender.

How It Works

Under 11 U.S.C. Section 722, an individual Chapter 7 debtor can redeem tangible personal property (not real estate) that is intended for personal, family, or household use by paying the secured creditor the replacement value of the property. This is typically much less than the outstanding loan balance.

For example, if a debtor owes $12,000 on a car worth $7,000, redemption allows the debtor to pay $7,000 (the car's value) and keep the vehicle. The remaining $5,000 is treated as unsecured debt and discharged. However, the payment must be made in a single lump sum, which is the main obstacle.

Because few debtors have thousands of dollars in cash available during bankruptcy, several lenders specialize in redemption financing -- making loans specifically to fund redemption payments. These loans typically carry high interest rates (15-25%) but can still be advantageous when the vehicle is significantly underwater.

Key Facts

  • Available only in Chapter 7 for personal property (not real estate)
  • Debtor pays the current value of the collateral, not the full loan balance
  • Payment must be made in a single lump sum
  • Redemption financing is available from specialty lenders
  • The remaining loan balance above value is discharged as unsecured debt

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I redeem my house?

No. Section 722 redemption applies only to tangible personal property intended for personal, family, or household use. Real estate cannot be redeemed under this section. Home retention in Chapter 7 requires reaffirmation or continued payment.

Where do I get the money for a lump sum payment?

Options include family loans, redemption financing companies (722 Redemption, Fresh Start Lending), savings, or tax refunds. Redemption financing carries high interest rates but can save thousands compared to the original loan balance.

Is redemption better than reaffirmation?

If you owe significantly more than the vehicle is worth, redemption is usually better -- you pay the lower value rather than the full loan balance. If you are close to equity, reaffirmation may be more practical since you avoid the lump sum requirement.
